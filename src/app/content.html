Cheat Sheet about Buddhism

Buddhism is all traditions of transmission, understanding, practice of dharma.

Schools of buddhism

  1. theravada
  2. chan
  3. zen

Informal practice, daily-life practice are synonyms.

1. Dharma

Dharma is teaching of liberation from dukkha.

Abhidharma is the highest dharma.

1.1. Dukkha

Dukkha is all kinds of unpleasent vedana.

Path leading to cessation of dukkha is noble eightfold path.

Conditions for occurrence of mental dukkha are mental object, grasping.

3 types of vedana are pleasent vedana, neutral vedana, unpleasent vedana.

1.1.1. Path

Path leading to cessation of dukkha is noble eightfold path.

Noble eightfold path is right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samadhi.

4 stages on path to awakening are sotapanna, sakadagami, anagami, arhat.

Right speech is no lying, no abusive speech, no backbiting, no talking nonsense.

Sotapanna is stream-enterer.

Sakadagami is once-returner.

Anagami is non-returner.

1.1.1.1. 4 noble truths

4 noble truths

  1. there is dukkha
  2. there is origin of dukkha
  3. there is cessation of dukkha
  4. there is path leading to cessation of dukkha

12 aspects of 4 noble truths

  1. "This is the noble truth of suffering."
  2. "This noble truth of suffering should be completely understood."
  3. "This noble truth of suffering has been completely understood."
  4. "This is the noble truth of the origin of suffering."
  5. "This noble truth of the origin of suffering should be given up."
  6. "This noble truth of the origin of suffering has been given up."
  7. "This is the noble truth of the cessation of suffering."
  8. "This noble truth of the cessation of suffering should be realized."
  9. "This noble truth of the cessation of suffering has been realized."
  10. "This is the noble truth of the practice that leads to the cessation of suffering."
  11. "This noble truth of the practice that leads to the cessation of suffering should be developed."
  12. "This noble truth of the practice that leads to the cessation of suffering has been developed."

Path leading to cessation of dukkha is noble eightfold path.

1.1.1.1.1. Aspects

3 aspects of each noble truth are recognition, duty, realization.

1.1.1.2. Prajna

Prajna is right view, right thought.

Prajna is wisdom.

1.1.1.3. Samadhi

Samadhi is right effort, right mindfulness, right samadhi.

Samadhi is immersion, concentration.

5 hindrances of samadhi are greed, hate, dullness, restlessness, doubt.

1.1.1.4. Arya

Arya is noble.

Arya is a person who achived any of 4 stages on path to awakening.

1.1.1.4.1. Person

A person with great merit become buddha or cakravartin.

3 jewels are buddha, dharma, sangha.

Buddha Maitreya is a future buddha.

1.1.1.4.1.1. Buddhist

Buddhist is a person who practice buddhism.

Buddhaghosa is buddhist.

Thich Nhat Hanh is buddhist monk.

1.1.1.4.1.1.1. Lama

Lama is tibetian buddhist teacher.

Ole Nydahl is lama.

Garchen Rinpoche is lama.

1.1.1.4.1.1.2. Visuddhimagga

Visuddhimagga is path of purification.

Visuddhimagga is a treatise written by Buddhaghosa.

1.1.1.4.1.1.2.1. Visuddhimaggamahatika

Visuddhimaggamahatika is a commentary on Visuddhimagga.

Visuddhimagganidanakatha is a commentary on Visuddhimagga.

Paramatthamanjusa is a commentary on Visuddhimagga.

Atthakatha is commentary on canon.

1.1.1.4.1.1.2.1.1. Tika

Tika is commentary on atthakatha.

Anutika is commentary on tika.

1.1.1.4.1.1.2.1.2. Tipitaka

Tipitaka is pali canon.

Tipitaka is triple basket.

1.1.1.4.1.2. Cakravartin

Cakravartin is wheel-turning monarch.

7 treasures of cakravartin are the wheel, the elephant, the horse, the jewel, the woman, the householder, the commander.

1.1.1.5. Sila

Sila is right speech, right action, right livelihood.

Sila is ethics.

Right action is no killing, no stealing, no sexual misconduct.

1.1.2. 5 aggregates of grasping

5 aggregates of grasping are rupa, vedana, samjna, sankhara, vijnana.

Rupa is form.

Upadana is grasping.

Conditions for occurrence of mental dukkha are mental object, grasping.
