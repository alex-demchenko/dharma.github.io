<h1>Buddhism</h1>
<p>Buddhism is all traditions of transmission, understanding, practice of dharma.</p>
<p>Schools of buddhism are theravada chan zen.</p>
<p>Informal practice, daily-life practice are synonyms.</p>


<h2>1. Dharma</h2>
<p>Dharma is teaching of liberation from dukkha.</p>
<p>Abhidharma is the highest dharma.</p>


<h3>1.1. Dukkha</h3>
<p>Dukkha is all kinds of unpleasent vedana.</p>
<p>Path leading to cessation of dukkha is noble eightfold path.</p>
<p>Conditions for occurrence of mental dukkha are mental object, grasping.</p>
<p>3 types of vedana are pleasent vedana, neutral vedana, unpleasent vedana.</p>


<h4>1.1.1. Path</h4>
<p>Path leading to cessation of dukkha is noble eightfold path.</p>
<p>Noble eightfold path is right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samadhi.</p>
<p>4 stages on path to awakening are sotapanna, sakadagami, anagami, arhat.</p>
<p>Right speech is no lying, no abusive speech, no backbiting, no talking nonsense.</p>
<p>Sotapanna is stream-enterer.</p>
<p>Sakadagami is once-returner.</p>
<p>Anagami is non-returner.</p>


<h5>1.1.1.1. 4 noble truths</h5>
<p>4 noble truths are there is dukkha there is origin of dukkha there is cessation of dukkha there is path leading to cessation of dukkha.</p>
<p>Path leading to cessation of dukkha is noble eightfold path.</p>


<h5>1.1.1.2. Prajna</h5>
<p>Prajna is right view, right thought.</p>
<p>Prajna is wisdom.</p>


<h5>1.1.1.3. Samadhi</h5>
<p>Samadhi is right effort, right mindfulness, right samadhi.</p>
<p>Samadhi is immersion, concentration.</p>
<p>5 hindrances of samadhi are greed, hate, dullness, restlessness, doubt.</p>


<h5>1.1.1.4. Arya</h5>
<p>Arya is noble.</p>
<p>Arya is a person who achived any of 4 stages on path to awakening.</p>


<h6>1.1.1.4.1. Person</h6>
<p>A person with great merit become buddha or cakravartin.</p>
<p>3 jewels are buddha, dharma, sangha.</p>


<h6>1.1.1.4.1.1. Buddhist</h6>
<p>Buddhist is a person who practice buddhism.</p>
<p>Thich Nhat Hanh is buddhist monk.</p>


<h6>1.1.1.4.1.1.1. Lama</h6>
<p>Lama is tibetian buddhist teacher.</p>
<p>Ole Nydahl is lama.</p>
<p>Garchen Rinpoche is lama.</p>


<h6>1.1.1.4.1.2. Cakravartin</h6>
<p>Cakravartin is wheel-turning monarch.</p>
<p>7 treasures of cakravartin are the wheel, the elephant, the horse, the jewel, the woman, the householder, the commander.</p>


<h5>1.1.1.5. Sila</h5>
<p>Sila is right speech, right action, right livelihood.</p>
<p>Sila is ethics.</p>
<p>Right action is no killing, no stealing, no sexual misconduct.</p>


<h4>1.1.2. 5 aggregates of grasping</h4>
<p>5 aggregates of grasping are rupa, vedana, samjna, sankhara, vijnana.</p>
<p>Rupa is form.</p>
<p>Upadana is grasping.</p>
<p>Conditions for occurrence of mental dukkha are mental object, grasping.</p>
