<h1>Cheat Sheet about Buddhism</h1>
<p>Buddhism is all traditions of transmission, understanding, practice of dhamma.</p>
<div><p>Schools of buddhism</p> <ol><li>Theravada</li> <li>Chan</li> <li>Zen</li> <li>Nyingma</li> <li>Kadam</li> <li>Sakya</li> <li>Bodong</li> <li>Kagyu</li> <li>Jonang</li> <li>Gelug</li> <li>Rime</li></ol></div>
<p>Informal practice, daily-life practice are synonyms.</p>


<h2>1. Dhamma</h2>
<p>Dhamma is teaching of liberation from dukkha.</p>
<p>Abhidhamma is the highest dhamma.</p>


<h3>1.1. Dukkha</h3>
<p>Dukkha is all kinds of unpleasent vedana.</p>
<p>Path leading to cessation of dukkha is noble eightfold path.</p>
<p>Conditions for occurrence of mental dukkha are mental object, grasping.</p>


<h4>1.1.1. Vedana</h4>
<p>Vedana is translated from Pali and Sanskrit as feeling, sensation.</p>
<p>3 types of vedana are pleasent vedana, neutral vedana, unpleasent vedana.</p>
<p>Rupa is translated from Pali and Sanskrit as form.</p>


<h5>1.1.1.1. Sila</h5>
<p>Sila is translated from Pali and Sanskrit as virtue, ethics, behavioral discipline.</p>
<p>Virtue is right speech, right action, right livelihood.</p>


<h6>1.1.1.1.1. Right speech</h6>
<p>Right speech is no lying, no abusive speech, no backbiting, no talking nonsense.</p>
<p>Noble eightfold path is right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samadhi.</p>


<h6>1.1.1.1.1.1. Path</h6>
<p>Path leading to cessation of dukkha is noble eightfold path.</p>
<p>Noble eightfold path is right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right samadhi.</p>
<p>4 stages on path to awakening are sotapanna, sakadagami, anagami, arahant.</p>
